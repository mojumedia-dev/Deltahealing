**Content**:
Spravato is the brand name for **esketamine**, a nasal spray medication approved by the FDA in 2019. It works differently than traditional antidepressants by targeting NMDA receptors in the brain, which may help restore neural connections affected by depression.

**Key Benefits:**

### Section 8: FAQs

**Heading (H2)**: Frequently Asked Questions About Spravato

**Q: Is Spravato the same as ketamine?**  
A: Spravato contains esketamine, which is derived from ketamine but is not identical. Esketamine is the more potent form and is FDA-approved specifically for treatment-resistant depression.

**Q: Is Spravato addictive?**  
A: Spravato has a potential for abuse and misuse, which is why it's only available through certified treatment centers under medical supervision. When used as prescribed under professional care, the risk is minimized.

**Q: Can I drive after treatment?**  
A: No. You must arrange for a responsible adult to drive you home after each treatment session. Do not drive or operate machinery until the next day after a full night's sleep.

**Q: What are common side effects?**  
A: Common side effects include dissociation (feeling detached from reality), dizziness, nausea, increased blood pressure, and sedation. Most side effects occur during or shortly after dosing and resolve within 1.5-2 hours.

**Q: How quickly will I see results?**  
A: Many patients experience improvement within hours to days, though the full effect may take several weeks of regular treatment.

**Q: Do I still take my other antidepressants?**  
A: Yes. Spravato is used in conjunction with an oral antidepressant. Never stop taking prescribed medications without consulting your psychiatrist.
