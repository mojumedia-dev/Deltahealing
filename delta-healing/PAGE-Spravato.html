What is Spravato?

Spravato (esketamine) is an FDA-approved nasal spray medication for adults with treatment-resistant depression. It works differently than traditional antidepressants by targeting NMDA receptors in the brain, which may help restore neural connections affected by depression.

If you've tried multiple antidepressants without success, Spravato offers new hope for rapid relief.

How Spravato Works

Unlike SSRIs and SNRIs that take weeks to work, Spravato can provide relief within hours or days for many patients. It works by increasing glutamate activity in the brain, promoting the formation of new neural connections that depression may have damaged.

Who is Spravato For?

Spravato may be appropriate if you:

  • Have tried two or more antidepressants without adequate improvement
  • Have been diagnosed with treatment-resistant depression
  • Experience major depressive disorder with acute suicidal ideation (when used with an oral antidepressant)
  • Are looking for a rapid-acting treatment option
Important: Spravato is only available through certified treatment centers like Delta Healing Solutions. It must be administered under medical supervision and cannot be taken at home.

What to Expect During Your Spravato Treatment Session

Before Your Session:

  • Consultation with our psychiatric provider to ensure Spravato is right for you
  • Arrange for someone to drive you home (you cannot drive yourself)
  • Avoid food for at least 2 hours before treatment
  • Avoid liquids for at least 30 minutes before treatment

During Your Session:

  • You'll self-administer the nasal spray under our medical supervision
  • Relax in a comfortable monitoring area for at least 2 hours
  • Our staff will monitor your blood pressure and overall response
  • Many patients describe feeling relaxed or slightly dissociated during observation

After Your Session:

  • You'll be monitored until you're ready to leave safely
  • A designated driver must take you home
  • Most side effects (dizziness, nausea, dissociation) resolve within 1.5-2 hours
  • You can usually resume normal activities the next day

Common Side Effects

Common side effects include:

  • Dissociation (feeling detached from reality) - typically resolves within 1.5 hours
  • Dizziness
  • Nausea
  • Increased blood pressure
  • Sedation

Most side effects occur during or shortly after dosing and resolve before you leave our clinic. This is why medical supervision is required.

Safety & Important Information

Cannot Drive: You must arrange for a responsible adult to drive you home after each treatment. Do not drive or operate machinery until the next day after a full night's sleep.

Abuse Potential: Spravato has a potential for abuse and misuse, which is why it's only available through certified treatment centers under medical supervision.

Pregnancy: Tell your provider if you are pregnant, planning to become pregnant, or breastfeeding.

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is Spravato the same as ketamine?

A: Spravato contains esketamine, which is derived from ketamine but is not identical. Esketamine is the more potent form and is FDA-approved specifically for treatment-resistant depression.

Q: How quickly will I see results?

A: Many patients experience improvement within hours to days, though the full effect may take several weeks of regular treatment. This is significantly faster than traditional antidepressants which take 4-8 weeks.

Q: Do I still take my other antidepressants?

A: Yes. Spravato is used in conjunction with an oral antidepressant. Never stop taking prescribed medications without consulting your psychiatrist.

Q: Is Spravato addictive?

A: Spravato has a potential for abuse and misuse, which is why it's only available through certified treatment centers under medical supervision. When used as prescribed under professional care, the risk is minimized.
