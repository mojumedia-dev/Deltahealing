What is ADHD?

ADHD (Attention-Deficit/Hyperactivity Disorder) is a neurodevelopmental condition characterized by persistent inattention, hyperactivity, and/or impulsivity that interferes with daily functioning.

Many people with ADHD go undiagnosed for years, struggling unnecessarily. Proper diagnosis opens the door to effective treatment and support.

Signs You or Your Child May Have ADHD

Inattention symptoms:

  • Difficulty sustaining attention on tasks
  • Makes careless mistakes
  • Doesn't seem to listen when spoken to
  • Fails to finish tasks
  • Difficulty organizing tasks and activities
  • Avoids tasks requiring sustained mental effort
  • Loses things frequently
  • Easily distracted
  • Forgetful in daily activities

Hyperactivity/Impulsivity symptoms:

  • Fidgets or squirms
  • Difficulty staying seated
  • Runs/climbs excessively (children) or feels restless (adults)
  • Difficulty engaging in quiet activities
  • Always "on the go"
  • Talks excessively
  • Blurts out answers
  • Difficulty waiting turn
  • Interrupts or intrudes on others

What Does ADHD Testing Involve?

Our comprehensive ADHD evaluation includes:

  • Clinical Interview - Detailed discussion of symptoms and history
  • Standardized Assessment Tools - Validated ADHD rating scales and questionnaires
  • Cognitive Testing - Assessments of attention, concentration, and executive function
  • Computerized Tests - Objective measures of attention and impulsivity (when appropriate)
  • Collateral Information - Input from family members, teachers, or partners
  • Ruling Out Other Conditions - Depression, anxiety, and other issues can mimic ADHD

Adult ADHD Testing

Yes, we test adults for ADHD! Many adults discover they have ADHD later in life when struggling with:

  • Work performance and organization
  • Time management and procrastination
  • Relationship difficulties
  • Chronic forgetfulness
  • Difficulty completing tasks
  • Impulsive decisions

Adult ADHD testing can provide clarity and open pathways to effective treatment, improving quality of life significantly.

Cognitive Testing for Other Concerns

Beyond ADHD, we provide cognitive evaluations for:

  • Memory Problems - Assessing memory concerns and cognitive decline
  • Learning Disabilities - Identifying specific learning challenges
  • Executive Function Deficits - Planning, organization, and problem-solving skills
  • Processing Speed - How quickly information is processed
  • Brain Injury/Concussion - Cognitive effects of head trauma

How Long Does Testing Take?

Cognitive testing typically takes 2-4 hours depending on the complexity of the evaluation. Testing may be completed in one session or split across multiple appointments for younger children or those who fatigue easily.

What Happens After Testing?

  • Diagnostic Clarity - Clear diagnosis if ADHD or other conditions are present
  • Treatment Recommendations - Personalized suggestions for next steps
  • School/Work Accommodations - Documentation for needed supports

Treatment Options for ADHD

If ADHD is diagnosed, we offer comprehensive treatment including:

  • Medication Management - Stimulants (Adderall, Ritalin, Vyvanse) and non-stimulant options (Strattera, Intuniv)
  • Behavioral Strategies - Organization, time management, coping skills
  • Therapy Referrals - CBT and other evidence-based therapies
  • Ongoing Monitoring - Regular follow-ups to optimize treatment
  • School/Work Accommodations - Support for needed adjustments
